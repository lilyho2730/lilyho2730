Adventure Report

Under the gleaming sun, I can smell the sunscreen lingering in the air. I walked towards the surfing kiosk on Condado Beach in San Juan, Puerto Rico, feeling the warm sand against my feet. A few other young girls were walking towards the glimmering shore, ready to launch themselves into the sea.

"Three?", asked the strong and muscular coach who is unbeatably hot and muscular.

"Do you guys have any prior experience?"

"No… "It's our first time," I murmured.

"Don’t worry at all; I will guide you all. Come on! Let’s hit the waves!"

I was flabbergasted by how hyped the coach is. Even though it has been a year since I wanted to do this, I was still a bit worried looking at the stench of waves. Nevertheless, I kicked off my sandals, dropped my surfboard onto the water, and I began my journey. As I approached the shore of the ocean, I saw the violent waves shaking the water. A sense of fear rushed over me, but I told myself everything was going to be fine.

Lay down, look forward, and keep your legs straight. When the wave hits, stand up and balance yourself. Sounds easy, right? I cuffed my leg with the board's leash, hoping for the best.

Pushing my surfboard to the middle of the sea, I stood and waited for the waves to hit.

Finally, the first wave came. I climbed on the board, laid with my legs straight, as the waves started to push my surfboard towards the shore. The waves were pushing the surfboard so fast that I felt like I was on an ocean rollercoaster.

I tried over 30 times to stand up on the surfboard. However, I failed to stand up every single time when I try to. I lost my balance and fell into the ocean with my nose and mouth stuffed with salty sea water that makes me so thirsty that I wanted some tap water to drink immediately. As the sea water filled my nostrils and throat, I couldn't stop coughing and sneezing.

After coughing so loudly for five minutes straight where everyone could hear me so clearly, I turned around to look at the shore. Looking back on the crowded beach, I felt embarrassed, as if I had performed a failure in front of 30 people who were sunbathing on the beach. Eyes were staring at me as if everyone thought that I was a loser who couldn’t balance at all. To prevent myself from digging a hole on the beach and hiding in it, I decided to look away to avoid embarrassment.

I calmed myself down and tried to focus. I told myself that if I don't stand up successfully, I'm not leaving this beach. Half blinded by the sea water, a strong wave was coming from a far distance. While hoping that it wouldn’t knock me off my surfboard, I took a deep breath and climbed onto the board, waiting for the waves to hit. Finally, after lifting my right foot up, my left foot gripped the board firmly, so I could stand up and balance my entire body. I threw my hands in the air and yelled a scream of joy.

Reaching the goal of my day, I left the water with a sore body. I went to pick up my camera, and I brushed off some of the sand that had gone into the lens. I walked to the shore and looked at my friend, who was also trying to balance on the fierce wave. I decided to capture the moment using my film camera. The one thing that I liked about a film camera is that you don’t get to see the picture you take immediately after you

Lying on the surfboard, floating in the ocean, is the best feeling I can have in this world. Suddenly, everything becomes so unimportant and insignificant. What matters the most is the present moment when I feel so alive, so free and so relaxed. Thinking back on the worries I had before, they suddenly seem so trivial that they are not worth thinking about. What matters most is to enjoy the present and live in the present.

I ended my day leaving the sinking sunset behind me with a radiant smile, making my way out of the beach. Facing the boundless ocean and hearing the pulse of the sea hitting the rocks at the shore, I felt like I was living my life to the fullest.
